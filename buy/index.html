Common Questions

License FAQ

How does the license key work?

After purchase, you will receive a license key by email. Enter it in TASS when prompted on first launch. The key is tied to your account and can be activated on up to two machines under the individual plan.

What happens when my license expires?

When your annual license expires, TASS enters a read-only mode: you can open and export existing projects, but you cannot run new analyses. Renewing your license restores full access immediately.

Is there an educational or student discount?

Yes. Verified students and early-career researchers (.edu email) can contact us at ap@simdadllc.com to request a discounted rate. Institutional site licenses also offer substantial per-seat savings over individual pricing for labs and departments.

Can I pay using a grant or purchase order?

Yes. Institutional licenses support invoice-based billing, which is compatible with most university grant accounts and departmental purchase processes. Contact us to initiate a quote.

Does a license include the T-Lex native dictionaries?

T-Lex dictionaries are developed independently of the TASS license and are freely available to all users — including non-licensed users — for academic and non-commercial research. They are not behind a paywall.

What operating systems does TASS support at launch?

TASS v1.0 launches as a native Windows application. Linux support is planned for v1.1 (Fall 2026). Mac support is under evaluation for a future release.

Is there a free trial?

TASS will include a 14-day trial period on first installation that provides full access to all features. No license key or credit card required to begin the trial.

What is your refund policy?

If TASS does not work as described on your system, contact us within 30 days of purchase for a full refund, no questions asked.
